Goals of Tutoring

Tutors should help students:

      

  • Understand their course content

  • Improve their reading and writing skills

  • Help students become self-sufficient

  • Identify their strengths and weaknesses

Setting the Agenda

    

Promote Independent Learning

  • Support students on becoming self sufficient.

    

Costumer Service

  • Convey knowledge through excellent customer service skills.

    

Learning Atmosphere

  • Create a positive learning atmosphere free of stress and other distractions.

    

Set up the Learning Space

  • Appropriate seating arrangement

  • Appropriate noise level

  • Areas free of clutter

  • No socializing while tutoring

    

Be Attentive

  • Look directly at the student and pay close attention

  • Ask questions to clarify information items

  • Repeat important information conveyed by the student

    

Enthusiastic

  • Smile and greet students

  • Introduce yourselves

  • Reinforce positive non-verbal communication throughout the session

    

Identify and Assign Tasks

  • Identify and assign tasks so students can practice and obtain immediate feedback during a tutoring session.

Conducting a Session

Active Listening:




Quick Tips for Active Listening

  • Make eye contact

  • Lean slightly toward the student

  • Guide students to make appropriate choices

  • Respond with appropriate facial expressions

  • Relax when you are watching the students communicate

      

Paraphrase what the student communicated

  • Ask questions that will lead the student to her/his own solution

  • Offer constructive feedback rather than criticize

  • Guide students to make appropriate choices

  • Ask the student if there is anything else s/he can share

  • Be sympathetic to difficult situations.
